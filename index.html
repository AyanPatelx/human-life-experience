Before you knew language, you only knew feeling.
i. childhood The world was impossibly large. Every afternoon lasted a lifetime. You believed in everything.
and then, slowly — things began to mean something.
ii. becoming someone You learned to introduce yourself in useful ways. You confused movement with progress. Everyone seemed ahead of you.
iii. love & connection You began leaving pieces of yourself in other people. Some moments felt infinite while they lasted.
iv. the machine years You became reachable at all times. The days stopped introducing themselves. You were efficient. You were tired.
v. reflection There was a moment you stood at the edge of everything and the whole life played back like something borrowed. The stars were still there.
Most of it passed unnoticed. And still, it was beautiful.
